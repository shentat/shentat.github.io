GCD Calculator

Definition of GCD

  • it is the greatest common divisor of two or more integers, when at least one of them is not zero, is the largest positive integer that divides the numbers without a remainder.

  • for example, the GCD of 8 and 12 is 4.

Implementation of the GCD

gcd <- function(x,y) {
    r <- x%%y;
    return(ifelse(r, gcd(y, r), y))
}

input: 8 and 12 give a gcd of

gcd(8, 12)

## [1] 4
